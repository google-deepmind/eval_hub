Denote the corresponding centres by $O_1$, $O_2$, $O_3$, $O_4$. Due to the symmetries, the quadrilateral $O_1O_2O_3O_4$ is a rhombus. We then need to find $4O_1O_2$. Let $M$ be the point of the intersection of the diagonals of $ABCD$. Then $O_1MO_2$ is a right triangle with $O_1O_2$ its hypotenuse. We will compute its length by the Pythagorean theorem. 
 
 The triangle $O_1AM$ has a right angle at $A$ due to the tangency. Let $N$ be the intersection of the lines $O_1M$ and $AB$, which is the middle of the side $AB$. As the triangle is $O_1AM$, the triangles $AMN$ and $O_1NA$ are similar. Therefore
 $$NO_1 = \frac{AN \cdot AN}{MN} = \frac{900^2}{480}.$$
 Then 
 $$MO_1 = MN + NO_1 = 480 +  \frac{900^2}{480} = \frac{480^2 + 900^2}{480}.$$
 
 Analogously,
 $$MO_2 = \frac{480^2 + 900^2}{900}.$$
 Hence
 \begin{equation}\label{a1}
 4O_1O_2 = 4\sqrt{\left(\frac{480^2 + 900^2}{480}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{480^2 + 900^2}{900}\right)^2 }
 \end{equation}
 Note that $480^2 + 900^2 = 60^2(8^2+15^2) = 60^2 \cdot 17^2$. Moreover
 $$\frac{60^2}{480} = \frac{60}{8} = \frac{15}{2}$$
 and
 $$\frac{60^2}{900} = \frac{60}{15} = \frac{8}{2}.$$
 Hence from \eqref{a1} we have
 $$4O_1O_2 = 4\sqrt{\left(\frac{60^2\cdot 17^2}{480}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{60^2\cdot 17^2}{900}\right)^2} 
 = 4\sqrt{ \left(17^2\cdot \frac{15}{2}\right)^2 + \left(17^2\cdot \frac{8}{2}\right)^2 } $$
 $$
 = 2\cdot 17^2\sqrt{15^2 + 8^2} = 2\cdot 17^3 = 9826.
 $$
