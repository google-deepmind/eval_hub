Bearing in mind the formula for the area of the triangle in terms of its lengths, $r$ and $R$, it is clear that:
$$\frac{11+17+a}{2} \cdot r = \frac{11 \cdot 17 \cdot a}{4 R}$$
\noindent so:
$$2r \cdot R=\frac{11 \cdot 17 a}{28+a}=11 \cdot 17 - \frac{11 \cdot 17 \cdot 28}{28+a}.$$
\noindent Hence, we have that $28 +a|11 \cdot 17 \cdot 28$ and the only possibility is $28+a=44$ so $a=16$.
