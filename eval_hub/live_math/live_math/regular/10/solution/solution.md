Let $I_1$, $I_2$, $I_3$ be the centers of the respective circles. From the conditions we have $I_1I_2 = 30$, $I_1 I_3 = 40$, $I_2 I_3 = 50$. Therefore the triangle $I_1I_2I_3$ is a right triangle, of area $\frac{30\cdot 40}{2} = 600$. Assume without lose of generality that $A$, $B$, $C$ lie on the intervals $I_1I_2$, $I_1I_3$, $I_2I_3$, respectively.


The triangle $ABC$ is created from the triangle $I_1I_2I_3$ after cutting out the triangles $I_1AB$, $I_2AC$, $I_3BC$. We then need to determine their areas. Let $\alpha_1 = \angle I_2I_1I_3$, $\alpha_2 = \angle I_3I_2I_1$, $\alpha_3 = \angle I_2I_3I_1$. We have $\alpha_1 = 90^\circ$, $\sin\alpha_2 = 4/5$ and $\sin \alpha_3 = 3/5$. Therefore the area of $I_1AB$ is
$$\frac{I_1A \cdot I_1B}{2} = 50.$$
The area of $I_2AC$ is
$$\frac{I_2A \cdot I_2C \sin\alpha_2}{2} = 160$$
and the area of $I_3BC$ is
$$\frac{I_3B \cdot I_3C \sin\alpha_3}{2} = 270$$


Hence the area of $ABC$ is
$$600 - 50 - 160 - 270 = 120.$$
