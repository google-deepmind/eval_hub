If the sides of the triangle are $a$, $b$, $c$, so that the incircles tangent to $a$, $b$, $c$ sides have respectively radii $r_a$, $r_b$, $r_c$, then the area $S$ of the triangle can be expressed in four ways:
 $$\frac{r(a+b+c)}{2} = \frac{r_a(-a+b+c)}{2} =\frac{r_b(a-b+c)}{2} =\frac{r_c(a+b-c)}{2}.$$
 Then
 $$\frac{1}{r_a} + \frac{1}{r_b} + \frac{1}{r_c} = \frac{(-a+b+c) + (a-b+c) + (a+b-c)}{2S} = \frac{a+b+c}{2S} = \frac{1}{r}.$$
 Hence 
 $$\frac 1r = \frac{1}{48} + \frac{1}{72} + \frac{1}{144} =  \frac{6}{144} = \frac{1}{24}.$$
