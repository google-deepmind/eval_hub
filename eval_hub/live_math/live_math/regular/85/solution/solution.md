First of all, notice that
$$[AEF] = \frac{1}{3} [ABC].$$
Now, by Menelaus's theorem for the line $CD$ in triangle $ABE$, we have
$$\frac{AD}{DB} \cdot \frac{BC}{CE}\cdot \frac{EH}{HA} = 1.$$
This means
$$ \frac{HA}{EH} = \frac{2}{5} \cdot \frac{6}{3} = \frac{4}{5}.$$
Analogously for triangle $ABF$ we get
$$ \frac{GA}{FG} = \frac{2}{5} \cdot \frac{6}{1} = \frac{12}{5}.$$
From those equalities we have
$$\frac{AG}{AF} = \frac{12}{17}, \qquad \frac{AH}{AE} = \frac{4}{9}.$$
Hence
$$[AGH] = \frac{48}{153} \cdot [AEF].$$
Then
$$[EFGH] = \frac{105}{153} \cdot [AEF] = \frac{35}{153}[ABC].$$
Hence $[ABC] = \frac{153}{35}\cdot 315 = 153\cdot 9 = 1377$.
