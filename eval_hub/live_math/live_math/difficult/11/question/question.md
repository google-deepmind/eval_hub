 Consider the set $S$ of all the positive integers $1 \leq n \leq 10^{12}$. Consider the subset $S_1$ of $S$ formed by the numbers $n$ which can be expressed as:
$$n=m \cdot a^3 + r \cdot b^4 + s \cdot c^6$$
\noindent where $0 \leq m,r,s \leq 7$ are positive integers and $a,b,c$ are also positive integers. Calculate:
$$\lfloor \frac{|S_1|}{|S \setminus S_1|} \rfloor,$$
\noindent that is, the floor function (or greatest integer function) of: $$\frac{|S_1|}{|S \setminus S_1|}.$$
