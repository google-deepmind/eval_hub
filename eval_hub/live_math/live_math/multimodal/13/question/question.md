Definition. Let $S \subseteq \mathbb{N}$ be a subsemigroup. Its weight function $w: \mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}$ is defined by the formula $w(\ell)=2 \cdot \#\{s \in S \mid s<\ell\}-\ell$ for all $\ell \in \mathbb{N}$. It is extended to a function $W: \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0} \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}$ via the formula $W(x)=\max \{w(\lfloor x\rfloor), w(\lceil x\rceil)\}$ for all $x \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}$. Let $X_{n}=\left\{x \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0} \mid W(x) \leq n\right\}$.

We now associate to this data a tree $T(S)=(V, E)$ together with a function $\chi: V \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}$. The set $\{v \in V \mid \chi(v)=n\}$ corresponds bijectively to the set of connected components of the topological space $X_{n}$. Two vertices $u$ and $v$ are connected by an edge $e=\{u, v\} \in E$ if and only if $\chi(u)=n+1, \chi(v)=n$ and the component of $X_{n+1}$ corresponding to $u$ contains the component of $X_{n}$ corresponding to $v$.
