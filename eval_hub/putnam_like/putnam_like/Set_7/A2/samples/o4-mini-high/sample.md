**Solution.**

Let \(G\) be a finite multiplicative subgroup of \(\mathbb{C}^*\) with \(\lvert G\rvert\ge2\). Define
\[
S \;=\; \sum_{z\in G} z.
\]
Pick any element \(g\in G\) with \(g\neq1\).  Since multiplication by \(g\) permutes the elements of \(G\), we have
\[
g\,S \;=\; \sum_{z\in G} g\,z \;=\; \sum_{w\in G} w \;=\; S.
\]
Hence
\[
(g-1)\,S \;=\; g\,S - S \;=\; 0.
\]
But \(g\neq1\) in \(\mathbb{C}\) implies \(g-1\neq0\), so we conclude
\[
S \;=\; 0,
\]
as required.
