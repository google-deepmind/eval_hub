Let $x(t)$ be a solution of the system
 \begin{equation}\label{Row}
 \left\{
 \begin{array}{l}
 \dot{x}(t)=A(t)x , \\
 x(0)\neq 0,
 \end{array}
 \right.
 \end{equation}
 where
 \begin{equation*}
 A(t)=\begin{pmatrix}
 1+\sin^2 t & -1 & -1 \\
 1 & 1/(1+\sin^2 t) & -1 \\
 1 & 1 & 1+\cos^2 t
 \end{pmatrix}.
 \end{equation*}
 Show that the function $\mathbb{R}\ni t \mapsto \|x(t)\|\in \mathbb{R}$ is
 increasing, where $\|\cdot\|$ denotes the Euclidean norm.
